Yes, it does! To be as brief as possible, usually the taller you are the more time it will take you to build muscles. A rare win for all the short kings out there.
But let's get into the details now: There is actually no significant effect that height has on muscle growth. What actually makes it look like so is that the shorter you are, the less muscle volume you'll need to fill (for example; taller limbs = more muscle).
Moreover, the shorter you are, the more height-to-width ratio you'll have, which explains why 2 equally wide people with different heights seem to have the shorter one more buff and muscular.
So to keep things short, no pun intended, the appearance and the time it takes for results to show may vary according to your height. Yet muscle growth does not depend on it.

Nutrition is actually the biggest factor in your bodybuilding progress! You should know that you're better off skipping weightlifting if you're not eating good food, whether you're cutting(losing weight) or bulking(gaining weight).
This is primarly because of the fact that your body requires all macronutrients (proteins, carbohydrates, and fats) and micronutrients (vitamins and minerals) for it to function normally, let alone the intensive activities and the muscle growth.
For example, having a deficit in protein will cause your muscles to be at risk for way more injuries, need more time to recover from soreness and inflammation, and also stay at its same mass/size if not lose some. Learn more about protein deficiency's results.
And protein is only one example of the many problems having an unbalanced diet causes... Which is why we recommend you do research about what you should eat and how much! Keep reading about nutrition in the gym.
